## Ingredients
- 1.3 kg boneless pork belly, skin on
- 30 g salt
- 5 g white pepper or black pepper
- 8 g sugar
- 6 g garlic powder
- 5 g Chinese five spice
- 15 ml white vinegar
- 15 ml neutral oil
- 12 jumbo tortilla wraps
- 435 g refried beans
- 375 g salsa
- 420 g sweetcorn kernels, drained
- 30 g coriander, roughly chopped
- 200 g cheese, grated, optional
- 250 g sour cream, optional
- 300 g avocado flesh, from about 2 avocados
- 120 g tomato, finely diced
- 15 g jalapeño, finely chopped
- 100 g onion, very finely diced

## Method
1. Preheat the oven to 110°C and line a roasting tray with foil.
2. Place the pork belly skin-side down and score the meat side in one direction about three-quarters of the way through, making sure not to cut through the skin.
3. Turn the pork belly 90 degrees and score again to create a crosshatch pattern on the meat side.
4. Mix 24 g of the salt with the pepper, sugar, garlic powder and Chinese five spice in a bowl.
5. Rub the seasoning mixture all over the meat side and into the cuts, keeping the skin as clean as possible.
6. Place the pork belly meat-side down on a large sheet of foil and fold the foil up around the sides to create a wall, leaving the skin fully exposed.
7. Brush the skin with the white vinegar and sprinkle the remaining 6 g salt over the top.
8. Roast the pork belly for 60 minutes, or until the skin feels dry and leathery.
9. Remove the pork belly from the oven, brush the skin lightly with the neutral oil, and increase the oven temperature to 205°C.
10. Return the pork belly to the oven and roast for 35 minutes, watching closely, until the skin is puffed, golden and very crisp.
11. Rest the pork belly for 10 minutes, then cut it into bite-sized pieces along the scored lines.
12. Mash the avocado in a bowl and mix with the tomato, jalapeño, onion and a little of the coriander to make the guacamole.
13. Heat the refried beans in a saucepan over low heat until hot.
14. Warm the sweetcorn until heated through and warm the tortilla wraps until soft and flexible.
15. Spread refried beans down the centre of each wrap, then add crispy pork belly, guacamole, sweetcorn, salsa, remaining coriander, cheese and sour cream.
16. Fold in the sides, roll tightly into burritos, and serve immediately.
17. Toast the rolled burritos in a dry frying pan for 2 minutes per side if you want the outside lightly crisped as well.

## Notes
The key to crispy skin is making sure it feels dry and leathery after the first oven stage. If it still feels damp, return it to the oven for another 10 minutes before the hot roast. Store the pork belly separately from the cold fillings for the best texture. Leftover pork reheats best in the oven or air fryer.

## Ingredients
- 800 g canned chickpeas, drained, reserve 80 ml liquid
- 125 ml tahini
- 80 ml lemon juice
- 10 g garlic, peeled
- 60 ml olive oil, plus extra to serve
- 2 g ground cumin
- 2 g salt
- 80 ml aquafaba, reserved from the chickpea cans
- 2 g paprika, to serve
- 10 g parsley, finely chopped, to serve

## Method
1. Drain the chickpeas and reserve 80 ml of the can liquid.
2. Add the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, cumin and salt to a blender or food processor.
3. Pour in the reserved aquafaba.
4. Blend until very smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides or using a tamper as needed.
5. Add a little more aquafaba if needed to loosen the hummus to your preferred consistency.
6. Spoon the hummus onto a plate or into a bowl and swirl the top with the back of a spoon.
7. Drizzle with a little extra olive oil and sprinkle with paprika and parsley before serving.

## Notes
Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. This is good with crackers, pita, veggie sticks, sandwiches or wraps.
